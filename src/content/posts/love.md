For most of a year, we were given the experiment we'd never have run on
ourselves: what does a life feel like with the public stripped out? The
answers turned out to be uneven, embarrassing, occasionally beautiful.

## The room as mirror

A room you cannot leave starts to act like a mirror. You can refuse to look —
many of us did — but the room keeps reflecting. Vulnerability stops being a
choice and becomes a condition.

## What love asked

Love, in that condition, asks for less performance and more attention. It
asks for the dishes, the dull Tuesday, the second cup of coffee. None of which
photograph well. All of which are the work.
